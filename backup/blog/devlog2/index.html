Sinister Santa and the road ahead (Devlog #2)

January 11th, 2022

Hey nerds. First off, some fun news - I’ve graduated college! This previous fall marked my last quarter at Savannah College of Art and Design. The past four-ish years have been an absolute blast. In getting a game design degree, I had the chance to work with so many fantastic peers and professors in creating a number of challenging, fun projects. I also got to dabble in a few other areas from creative writing to astronomy, picking up some additional interesting knowledge and experiences along the way.

Graduating is truly a bittersweet feeling. I’m extremely excited to be moving on to start my career in earnest, though I will miss the freedom and familiarity of student life. But as the Shire’s best poet once say, the road goes ever on and on, and I must follow if I can!

If your team’s looking for a System Designer to work on conjuring up cool stuff in Unreal Engine, let me know! I’m on the hunt for a full time position and would love for us to strike up a conversation.

SINISTER SANTA

The holidays gifted me with inspiration for a side project I’ve been working on a few weeks now - Sinister Santa, the unsettling answer to the never-before-asked question of “what would you do if the big man in red wanted you dead?” It’s a short, whimsical shoot-‘em-up with arcade-like mechanics and a creepy Christmas coating. From the confines of your snow fort, defend yourself from the oncoming horde of elves, reindeer, and snowmen that Santa’s sent to annihilate you. Stick it out long enough to duel St. Nick himself and earn your place on the naughty list for good!

I’m onto the final stages of testing and polish and aim to have the game out within the week*. It’ll be free (with optional donation if you’re still in a giving spirit) on Itch.io, so follow me there to be the first to know as soon as it’s out. I’ve had a ton of fun making this and am looking forward to y’all playing this with all the excitement of a kid waiting for Christmas morning!

*I’m optimistic about this, but I’ve already been quite delayed on this due to, well, <gestures broadly at the world around him>.

LET’S MAKE SOME GAMES

Thanks for bearing with me over the holiday hiatus! Life’s a bit weird at the moment while I figure out a job, future living situation, and wrap up Sinister Santa, but I should settle into a proper routine sooner or later. Moving forward, the goal is to post a weekly devlog about my main project(s) and more specific articles whenever I have something interesting to ramble about. Follow me on Twitter (@mvpgamedev) for more consistent posts (both serious and silly) and definitely reach out if you want to work on something together - mpoff99 (at) gmail (dot) com for email, and I’m happy to give you my Discord if you ask. Enjoy the new year, keep safe, play some cool games, and, most critically, stay weird!
